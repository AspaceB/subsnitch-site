About SubSnitch

Every day, millions of people unknowingly sign up for subscriptions they never wanted. A pre-checked checkbox here, a "free trial" that auto-renews there, fine print designed to be invisible. These aren't accidents — they're carefully engineered dark patterns built to extract money from people who aren't paying close enough attention.

We think that's wrong.

The Problem

Dark patterns in subscription flows have become an epidemic. Companies spend millions optimizing their checkout pages — not to make them clearer, but to make them more deceptive. Pre-checked boxes that add recurring charges. "Free trial" buttons in large text with "$49.99/month after 3 days" in 8px gray-on-gray font. Cancellation flows that require phone calls during narrow business hours.

The FTC has taken action against some of the worst offenders, but regulation moves slowly. Consumers need protection now.

The Solution

SubSnitch is a Chrome extension that acts as your personal dark pattern detector. It scans checkout pages in real time, analyzing the DOM for known deceptive patterns: pre-checked subscription boxes, hidden recurring charges in fine print, misleading "free" offers, and more.

When SubSnitch finds something suspicious, it shows you a clear, prominent warning — before you click "Buy." No more discovering mystery charges on your credit card statement.

"The best way to cancel an unwanted subscription is to never sign up for it in the first place."

Our Principles

Privacy first. SubSnitch processes everything locally in your browser. We don't collect data, run analytics, or track you. Your browsing is your business.

Free forever. Consumer protection shouldn't be a premium feature. SubSnitch is and will always be free to use.

Open source. Every line of code is public. Audit it, fork it, contribute to it. Transparency isn't optional — it's the foundation.
